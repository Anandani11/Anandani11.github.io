    • What is this?

    To create a basic layout of a blog some basic page positioning codes have to be used in order to justify the positioning of the content and boxes that make up a blog. The content must have justified margins, borders and padding in order for it to be visually appealing. When content is positioned correctly it enables the user to easily read, digest and use the content of the blog. Margins, borders and padding are helpful when trying to portray information in a certain way and can change the look and feel of a blog. Example take a nice juicy Chicken Burger, the patty inside the burger is your content, the extras so your cheese, sauce and salad is your padding, the hamburger bun is your border box and your Margin is the hamburger box.

    How and When do you use this?

    • Margin

      • This is what you can use to separate your content. This creates boxes between your paragraphs.

    • Border

      • This is the outline box around your content, you can use padding to create space between your content making this look like a box.

    • Padding

      • This is the space between your content and border box, you would use this to make your content look clear and tidy.
